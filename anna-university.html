Understanding the Anna University Attendance Rule

For engineering departments, constituent campuses, and affiliated autonomous/non-autonomous institutions under the university banner, the Anna University attendance rule is extremely strict. Under both Regulation 2017 and Regulation 2021, students must maintain a minimum of 75% overall attendance to qualify for end semester examinations.

Our 75% attendance calculator helps you compute how many sessions you can skip while remaining eligible. Simply input your current classes conducted and classes attended to get immediate results.

What Happens in Case of an Attendance Shortage?

If you drop below the 75% limit but maintain at least 65% attendance, you fall under the condonation bracket. Getting condoned is subject to Principal/HOD approval and involves:

  • Providing medical certificates (in case of illness) within the deadline.
  • Submitting On-Duty (OD) forms approved by the department for representing the college.
  • Paying a condonation fee.

If you drop below 65%, no condonation will be granted and you will be debarred from the examinations.

How to Find Out "How Many Classes Can I Miss"?

Using our attendance calculator college widget above, you can plan your absences. The program takes your current stats and computes your safe absences using standard division limits. Internal linking back to the main UniWise page lets you reset or compare your stats easily.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the debarment rule at Anna University?
Students with attendance below 65% are strictly debarred. They cannot write the semester exams and must register to repeat the semester in the next academic year.
How does UniWise help me track safe absences?
It uses the formula floor((100 * Attended) / Required %) - Conducted to identify how many upcoming sessions you can miss without dropping below the threshold.
