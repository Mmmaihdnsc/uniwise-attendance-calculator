The 75% Attendance Rule Regulation 2021

For all courses offered under Regulation 2021 and Regulation 2017, Anna University mandates a minimum of 75% attendance. This is calculated per course or as an overall aggregate depending on your college's policy. If your attendance drops below 75% but stays above 65%, you are in the condonation bracket. Shortages in this zone must be backed by official medical documents and require paying a condonation fee to release your hall ticket. Falling below 65% results in debarment, requiring you to repeat the semester courses next year.

How is Attendance Calculated in Engineering Colleges?

In all colleges governed by Anna University, your department records attendance in hours. If your class has conducted 100 hours of lectures, you must be present for at least 75 hours to be completely safe. Frequently Asked Questions

How many classes can I miss in Anna University?
Under the 75% attendance rule, you can safely miss up to 25% of the total sessions. If 100 hours are conducted, you can safely miss 25 hours. If you are already at 75%, you cannot miss any more.
What happens if attendance is below 75%?
If your attendance is between 65% and 74%, you must pay a condonation fee and present medical or sports OD proof to get your hall ticket. Below 65%, you are debarred from writing exams.
How is attendance calculated in engineering colleges?
Your college attendance is calculated as: (Total attended hours / Total conducted hours) * 100. Our tool computes this in real-time.
