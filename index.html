Understanding Your Attendance Analysis

Managing college life requires keeping a close eye on your academic statistics. The UniWise dashboard provides an active check of your class metrics so you can make informed choices about your time:

  • Current Attendance Percentage: This represents the percentage of total conducted hours you have successfully attended. If this is above your required threshold, your status shows "Safe".
  • Safe Absences: This indicates how many classes you can safely miss starting from today without letting your overall percentage drop below the required limit.
  • Classes to Attend: If your current attendance falls below the target, this displays the number of consecutive, upcoming sessions you must attend to pull your average back to safety.

How is Attendance Calculated in Engineering Colleges?

Academic institutions across Tamil Nadu utilize a standard contact-hour system. The formula to calculate your percentage is basic division:

Attendance % = (Hours Attended ÷ Total Hours Conducted) × 100

For example, if your branch has conducted 80 hours of lectures and labs, and you were present for 64 hours, your average is 80%. If the college follows a 75% attendance rule, you can calculate your margin of safe absences to determine how many classes can I miss.

General Attendance Rules in Tamil Nadu Institutions

Under regulations laid down by the Directorate of Technical Education (DoTE) and local affiliated universities, students must secure a minimum of 75% attendance to qualify for terminal exams. If you secure between 65% and 74%, a condonation may be granted upon submission of medical records or official On-Duty (OD) letters and payment of a fee. Falling below 65% results in debarment.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many classes can I miss in college Tamil Nadu?
This depends on your total classes conducted. Under a standard 75% rule, you can miss up to 25% of all conducted sessions. For example, if you have 100 sessions, you can safely miss 25.
What happens if my attendance falls below 75%?
If your attendance is between 65% and 74%, you must pay a condonation fee and present medical or OD proof to receive your hall ticket. Below 65%, you are debarred and must repeat the semester.
How is attendance calculated in engineering colleges?
Attendance is calculated by dividing your total hours present by the total hours conducted by the department, multiplied by 100. Our tool computes this live.
